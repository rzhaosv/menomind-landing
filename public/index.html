What if it's not anxiety, stress, or "just aging"?

Over 80% of women experience symptoms of perimenopause — the hormonal transition that can start in your mid-30s, years before menopause. It shows up as brain fog, anxiety, rage, insomnia, fatigue, weight gain, and dozens of other symptoms that get blamed on stress, aging, or "just being a woman." Most doctors receive less than a few hours of menopause training in medical school. That means the person most likely to figure out what's happening to you... is you.

What is perimenopause? +
Perimenopause is the transition period leading up to menopause, when your ovaries gradually produce less estrogen and progesterone. This typically lasts 4-10 years and can start in your 30s or 40s. During this time, hormone fluctuations often cause symptoms like hot flashes, mood changes, sleep disruption, and brain fog — which are sometimes misdiagnosed as anxiety or depression.
