Questions you're probably asking yourself

Could this really be perimenopause? +
If you're between 35 and 55 and something feels different — yes, it could be. Perimenopause can start a full decade before menopause, and most women have no idea. It's not just hot flashes. It's anxiety that appears out of nowhere, brain fog that makes you question yourself, rage you can't explain, insomnia, weight gain, and dozens of other symptoms that are often misdiagnosed as stress, anxiety, or depression. You're not imagining it.
Is what I'm feeling normal? +
Yes. What you're experiencing is far more common than you've been told. Research shows over half of women don't feel adequately informed about perimenopause symptoms. The sudden anxiety, the brain fog, the disrupted sleep, the mood changes — millions of women go through this. You are not broken, you are not alone, and there are explanations for what's happening in your body.
My doctor says I'm too young for perimenopause. Is that true? +
This is the single most common complaint we hear — and it's often wrong. Medical research confirms that perimenopause can begin in the mid-to-late 30s. More than a third of women experiencing perimenopause symptoms go undiagnosed, and many are told their symptoms are "just stress" or that they're "too young."
